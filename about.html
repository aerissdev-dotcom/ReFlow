Why ReFlow exists

Built for the days that are actually hard.

Most recovery tools were designed like spreadsheets. ReFlow was designed like a place you'd actually want to open.

Less shame. More visible progress. Support exactly when it's needed.

The problem

Most sobriety and habit-tracking apps reduce recovery to a single cold number - a streak. It climbs quietly for weeks, and then one hard day resets it to zero. That all-or-nothing framing doesn't just feel discouraging; it's the exact moment people abandon the app altogether, losing the one tool that could have actually helped.

On top of that, most of these apps are clinical and silent. They log your data but offer nothing back in the moment a craving actually hits - no visual sense of progress, no outlet, no encouragement. You're left tracking recovery entirely alone, which is precisely when support matters most.

ReFlow was built to fix both problems at once: make progress feel real instead of numeric, and make support available the instant it's needed.

Three ways ReFlow improves your day-to-day

R

Progress you can actually feel

A number is easy to ignore. A river that visibly widens and fills with algae and fish over time isn't - it turns abstract days clean into something you can watch grow, which makes the effort feel worth showing up for.

S

No shame resets

A slip makes the river recede a little - it doesn't erase it. You keep your history instead of starting over from a blank slate, so one hard day doesn't take away your reason to keep going tomorrow.

C

Support the moment it's needed

Cravings don't wait for business hours. The companion is there at 2am as easily as at noon, giving you somewhere to go before an urge turns into a decision you regret.

A genuine quality-of-life improvement

Recovery is hard enough without your own tracker working against you. ReFlow replaces guilt-driven streaks with visible, forgiving progress and support that's always within reach - small shifts that make staying the course meaningfully easier, day after day.
